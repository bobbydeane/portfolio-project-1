About our tour

peace lines

Visit and write a message on most visible sign of the divisions that have scarred the city for so long, the so-called 'Peace Line' that divides West Belfast's Protestant and Catholic communities. The 8 metre high walls of concrete and corrugated steel have been in place since 1969 and have outlasted the Berlin Wall.

Local guides.

Be guided through the Infamous Falls Road by a Former Republican Prisoner and see imagery of the communities desire to be a part of an Irish Republic. The pass through the gates of the peace line to the Loyalist Shankhill Road, where you will be met by a Loyalist Prisoner. Here you will learn of of the local communities determination to remain a part of the United Kingdom.

politcal murals

There have been over 2000 murals in Belfast since the 1970s and each offer a colourful insight into the citys past. In recent years the Murals have become less militaristic in nature, but some glorifying the citys paramilitary organisations remain. You will learn how Murals of West Belfast are a visceral reminder of the tensions that once tour the city apart.

International Wall

Stop off at the world famous 'International Wall' on the falls road. Known as the worlds largest outdoor art gallery, the wall and surrounding area boast various Murals commemorating different 'struggles' from around the world. From Cuban revoloutionaries to Catalan separatists, Black lives matter and beyond.
